North American Tour 2000

Masterworks, Yes' summer tour of North America, is now underway with Kansas sharing the bill. For this tour the band will be performing songs that have not been played on stage in recent years, including "epic masterpieces".

Recently a poll was conducted on YesWorld asking what songs fans regarded as Yes Masterworks. The results were considered by the band in compiling their set list, which is slated to include "The Gates of Delirium" (the poll winner), "Ritual", and "Close to the Edge" (both in the top 5).

"It turned out that the fans really wanted to hear the more epic side of the band, including songs such as ‘Gates Of Delirium’ which we haven't performed in many years,” says Jon Anderson. “It just so happens that we were moving in this direction as well and it allows us to honor the passion and loyalty of our fans while presenting new musical challenges to us as a group.”

Billy Sherwood Departs Yes

Billy Sherwood, a member of Yes since 1997, has left the group to focus on other creative endeavors. Billy's departure is on amicable terms with the group and his exit was agreed to by mutual consent. 

According to the band, “We support Billy in his decision and wish him all the best in the future.” Yes will tour with the classic line-up of Jon Anderson, Chris Squire, Steve Howe, and Alan White, who are once again joined by Igor Khoroshev.

"Close to the Edge - The Story of Yes"

The Yes bio "Close to the Edge - The Story of Yes" has been updated to include the recording of the group's 1999 The Ladder album and subsequent tour.

Penned by English rock journalist Chris Welch, is a comprehensive biography on Yes. Welch, who is best known for his long career at Melody Maker as well as authoring other bios (including Jimi Hendrix and Pink Floyd), has drawn on his longtime association with Yes to tell the story of the band's evolution from the early days right to the present. Every musician who's been a band member has been interviewed for this book as well as friends and associates.

The book is 295 pages and includes many photographs. It is published by Omnibus press and is available direct from Music Sales in the UK.

New RollingStone.com
Yes Documentary Installments

The latest installments of the Yes documentary YES 1.0: 1968-1980 at RollingStone.com cover the period from Time and a Word through Yessongs. In addition to the Yes albums there are new features on individual band members, including Steve Howe and former band mate Rick Wakeman.
