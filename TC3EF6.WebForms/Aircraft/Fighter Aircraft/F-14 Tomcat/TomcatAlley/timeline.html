1961

AUG
  • The Tactical Fighter Experimental (TFX) program was initiated.

1965

MAY
  • 15th. The first F-111B makes its maiden flight.

1967

OCT
  • Seeing the hand-writing on the wall for F-111B, Grumman brings unsolicited proposal to VADM Thomas Connolly.
NOV
  • Defense Secretary Robert McNamara announces his resignation.

1968

MAR
  • 1st. Defense Secretary Robert McNamara leaves his post.
  • 4th. VADM Connolly testifies before the Senate Armed Services Committee on the inadequacies of F-111B.
MAY
  • Senate elects to kill F-111B and gives the go-ahead to the VFX program.
JUN
  • 21st. The Request for Proposal for the VFX is issued to the American defense industry.
JUL
  • 17th. Five companies are awarded the VFX Concept Definition Contracts.
OCT
  • 1st. Initial proposals are submitted from the five bidders.
DEC
  • 13th. Proposal evaluations are completed.
  • 17th. Grumman and McDonnell Douglas are announced as the finalists to the program.

1969

JAN
  • 14th. Grumman wins the contract.
FEB
  • 3rd. Grumman is awarded a research, development, test, and evaluation contract.
MAY
  • 23rd. The mockup airframe of the F-14 is completed.
SEP
  • Grumman informs Navy of anticipated finalcial difficulties due to the fixed-price contract.

1970

DEC
  • 14th. F-14 prototype #1 begins taxi trials at Grumman's Calverton facility in New York.
  • 21st. Prototype #1 succesfully makes its first flight.
  • 30th. Prototype #1 crashes on its second flight due to malfunctioning hydraulic systems.

1971

JAN
  • Grumman executives meet with Navy top brass to discuss finalcial problems.
MAR
  • First production Pratt & Whitney TF30-P-412 engine is delivered.
APR
  • Grumman's problems are first reported by New York Times.
  • Navy F-14 program manager, CAPT Mike Ames, conducts press conference to address the money issue.
MAY
  • 24th. Five months after the original prototype's first flight, aircraft #2 takes to the air.
JUN
  • Unhappy with contraversies the program is generating, House of Representative eliminates $801 million appropriation for the F-14 in FY 1972.
SEP
  • Senate restores the $801 million previously slashed by the House.
OCT
  • Trials of integrating AWG-9/AIM-54 weapons system with the aircraft begin at PMTC Point Mugu, California.

1972

MAY
  • First production F-14A Tomcat is delivered.
APR
  • 28th. First live-firing of a Phoenix missile from a Tomcat takes place at Point Mugu.
JUN
  • The first Fleet Readiness Squadron, VF-124 Gunfighters, is established at NAS Miramar, California.
SEP
  • First P&W F401 engine is test flown in the #7 prototype (BuNo 157986).
OCT
  • First two operational F-14 squadrons, VF-1 Wolfpack and VF-2 Bounty Hunters, are established at NAS Miramar.

1973

MAR
  • Navy agrees to renegotiate contract after Lot V.
JUL
  • Shah of Iran visits the US and watches flight demonstrations by F-14 and F-15.
AUG
  • Iranian government formally requests information on the F-14 and F-15.
  • Grumman and the Navy agree to a new contract.
OCT
  • Shah of Iran receives DoD permission to purchase both F-14 and F-15.
NOV
  • 22nd. Operation Six-on-Six. During which a single F-14 engages six targets with six Phoenix missiles simultaneously.
DEC
  • First fleet squadrons (VF-1 and VF-2) become operational.
  • Iran signs order for 30 F-14s.

1974

APR
  • The Navy suspends the P&W F401 engine program due to technical difficulties and cost overruns.
JUN
  • Allegations of Navy's coverup to Grumman's demand for advance payment prior to Lot VII run rampant.
  • Iran orders 50 more Tomcats.
JUL
  • Navy changes terms of loans to Grumman.
AUG
  • Senate rejects navy's new agreement with Grumman.
  • The Iranian government-operated bank, Bank Melli, offers a loan of $75 million to Grumman
SEP
  • 17th. F-14A makes its operational debut in the Pacific Fleet with VF-1 and VF-2 aboard USS Enterprise (CVN-65).
OCT
  • Grumman negotiates credit lines with Bank Melli and a consortium of US banks.

1975

APR
  • Operation Frequent Wind. Tomcats of VF-1 and VF-2 fly top-cover for the evacuation of US personnel from Saigon.
JUN
  • F-14A makes its operational debut in the Atlantic Fleet with VF-14 Tophatters and VF-32 Swordsmen aboard USS John F. Kennedy (CV-67).

1976

JAN
  • The first of the 80 aircraft ordered is delivered to the Imperial Iranian Air Force.
APR
  • The development on the Tactical Airborne Reconnaissance Pod System commences.
SEP
  • 14th. A VF-32 Tomcat rolls off the deck of Kennedy.

1977

JAN
  • P&W TF30-P-414 engines are delivered.
SEP
  • Flight trials with the TARPS system begin.

1981

JUL
  • 14th. The General Electric F101 DFE (Derivative Fighter Engine) takes to the air for the first time in the F-14B prototype (BuNo 157986).
AUG
  • 3rd. TARPS system is deployed for the first time in the Atlantic fleet with VF-84.
  • 19th. VF-41 Black Aces becomes the first fighter squadron to blood the Tomcat in combat when two of its aircraft shot down two Libyan Su-22s over the Gulf of Sidra.
OCT
  • 20th. TARPS system is deployed for the first time in the Pacific fleet with VF-211.

1982

DEC
  • Navy decides to develop the F-14D Super Tomcat instead of procuring a brand new aircraft.

1983

AUG
  • P&W TF30-P-414A engines begin to be delivered.

1984

MAY
  • Navy and Grumman reach agreement on F-14D and A-6F program scope and cost.
JUL
  • Full scale development contract for the F-14D and the interim F-14A+ is signed.
OCT
  • MSIP radar is incorporated.
DEC
  • Dual chin-pod for InfraRed Search & Track and Television Camera Set systems is incorporated.

1985

OCT
  • 7th. The Achille Lauro incident.

1986

SEP
  • 29th. First GE F110 engine test flight takes place. Once again, prototype #7 (BuNo 157986) is used as the testbed.

1987

MAR
  • 31st. Last production F-14A (BuNo 162711, Block 140) is delivered.
NOV
  • 14th. First production F-14A+ (BuNo 162910, Block 145B) rolls off the assembly floor.

1988

JAN
  • Flight trials for the new avionics and radar systems.

1989

JAN
  • 4th. Two VF-32 Swordsmen F-14s splash two Libyan MiG-23s over the Mediterranean.

1990

FEB
  • 20th. Last production F-14A+ ((BuNo 163411, Block 155B) rolls off the factory floor.
MAR
  • 23rd. The first production F-14D is officially unveiled during a ceremony at Calverton, New York.
MAY
  • 23rd. First production F-14D (BuNo 163412, Block 160D) is delivered.
AUG
  • 2nd. F-14A+ makes it operational debut with VF-74 Bedevilers and VF-103 Sluggers aboard USS Saratoga (CV-60).

1991

JAN
  • 21st. One F-14B of VF-103 is shot down by an Iraqi SA-2 surface-to-air missile. The RIO is captured and taken prisoner by Iraqi troops whilst the pilot is eventually rescued by a USAF special forces team.
FEB
  • 6th. A VF-1 Tomcat scores one of the Navy's three air-to-air kills in the Persian Gulf War by shooting down an Iraqi Mi-8 helicopter.
MAR
  • Defense Secretary Dick Cheney kills the Super Tomcat program by deleting the fundings from FY 1992 budget.
MAY
  • 1st. All F-14A+s are redesignated F-14Bs by the Chief of Naval Operations.

1992

JUL
  • 10th. The last production F-14D (BuNo 164604, Block 170D) is delivered.

1993

MAR
  • The last of remanufactured F-14Ds enters service.

1994

FEB
  • 18th. F-14D Super Tomcat makes it operational debut with VF-11 Red Rippers and VF-31 Tomcatters aboard USS Carl Vinson (CVN-70).
MAY
  • With the cancellation of the A-6F and the F-14D programs, Grumman is forced to merge with Northrop and loses its independence.
NOV
  • A contractor team led by Lockheed Martin begins to work on a Navy adoptation of the LANTIRN (Low Altitude Navigation/Targeting InfraRed for Night) system in use by the USAF.

1995

MAR
  • The modified and upgraded LANTIRN system is integrated with an operational F-14.
SEP
  • 5th. VF-41 makes history again when a section of its Tomcats dropped four Mk-83 laser guided bombs over Bosnia. It was the first time ever an F-14 had dropped bombs in combat.
NOV
  • The Navy formally awards the contract for LANTIRN system to Lockheed Martin.

1996

JAN
  • VF-103 begins home trials with the LANTIRN system.
JUN
  • 14th. The LANTIRN pod is officially unveiled in a ceremony at NAS Oceana.
  • 28th. LANTIRN makes its operational debut with VF-103 Jolly Rogers as they embarked on their Mediterranean cruise aboard Enterprise.
