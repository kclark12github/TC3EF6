Tupolev Tu22M Backfire

Tu22M

The Tu22M is a medium range strike aircraft. There are about 100 avaliable to the Air Force for theater strike, the Naval Forces have 165 for anti-shipping and carrier task group attacking. The majority of these are the Tu22M3 (Backfire C) as opposed to the Tu22M2 (Backfire B). While the Tu22M is a completely different aircraft it has it's origions in the Tu22 Blinder, a look at an early Backfire prototype can see the resembelance. The Tu22 Blinder was dissapointing in bomb load, speed and range. The greatly changed Backfire was intended to address these shortcommings.

The Backfire is capable of nuclear strike, conventional attack and anti-ship missions. It is the first of the large Russian aircraft with low level penetration capability. This ability makes it a much more survivable aircraft than earlier Russian medium-heavy bombers such as the Tu22 Blinder, Tu16 Badger and M4 Bison.

Tu22M2 (Backfire B) This was the initial series production. Was fitted with the 48 500 lb thrust NK-22 turbofans. The engine intakes were slightly inclined and had large splitter plates. The wingsweep was variable from 20 degrees to 65 degrees. Armament was up to three of the early Kh-22 (AS-4 "Kitchen") missiles. These are massive missiles that deliver a large conventional warhead at high speed.. Alternatively the Tu22M2 is capable of carrying 46 300 lb of conventional bombs or mines. At the rear of the aircraft there is a radar directed tail gun similar to those on the B-52. It is a twin 23 mm turrent.

Tu22M3 (Backfire C) The Tu22M3 is a more advanced version that was deployed from 1983 onwards. The air intakes were modified into more of a wedge shape, also the engines were upgraded to a more powerful models. The nose was upturned and the maximum weapons load was also increased. The radar directed tail turrent was redesinged into a more aerodynamic unit with one gun ontop of the other instead of side by side. The Backfire C is also able to carry the Kh-15P (AS-16 "Kickback") SRAM. This has increased the total deliverable number of warheads.

There is also an Electronic Warfare version with a semirecessed EW centerline pod. There are also a number of extra fairings but as yet it is unknow exactly as to their purpose.
