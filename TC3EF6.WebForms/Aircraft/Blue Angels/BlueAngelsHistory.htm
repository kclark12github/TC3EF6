Chronology:

June 1946: F6F-5 Hellcat
At the end of World War II, Adm. Chester W. Nimitz, then the Chief of Naval Operations, ordered the formation of a flight demonstration team to keep the public interested in naval aviation. The Blue Angels performed their first flight demonstration less than a year later in June 1946 at their home base, Naval Air Station (NAS) Jacksonville, Florida. Flying the Grumman F6F Hellcat, they were led by Lt. Cmdr. Roy "Butch" Voris.
 
August 1946: F8F-1 Bearcat
The Blues change planes to the Grumman F8F Bearcat, and introduce the "Diamond" formation.

August 1949: F9F-2 Panther
The Blues again change planes, and usher in the Jet Age, Flying the Grumman F9F-2 Panther. The first show is flown at Beaumont, Texas.
August 1950: Active Duty
Formed the nucleus of VF-191 "Satan's Kittens" aboard the USS Princeton during the Korean Conflict.
Late 1951: F9F-5 Panther
The team is reassigned to demonstration duty, based out of NAS Corpus Christi, Texas. They upgrade aircraft to the Grumman F9F-5 Panther. Solo Pilots briefly fly the Vought F7U-1 Cutlass.
June 1952:
The new team flies its first show at the Memphis Midsouth Naval Festival.
 
February 1954:
The first Marine Corps pilot joins the team to fly on of the wing positions in the Diamond.
 
January 1955: F9F-8 Cougar
Another aircraft switch, this time to the Grumman F9F-8 Cougar.
 
September 1956:
The team flies its first demonstration outside the US, going north across the border to Canada.
 
May 1957: F11F-1 Tiger
Yet another aircraft change, as the team leaves the F9F behind, moving up to the Grumman F11F-1 Tiger.
July 4, 1963:
The team flies its 1000th demonstration at NAS Lemoore, California.
 
July 1965:
The Blues again leave the US. This time for their first ever European tour. The team visits France, England, Finland, Denmark, Holland and Iceland.
 
May and June 1967:
The team makes its second European tour, this time with stops in Italy, Tunisia, Turkey, and France.
 
December 1968: F-4J Phantom II
The Blues change aircraft manufacturers, with the McDonnell Douglas F-4J Phantom II.

1970:
The first Lockheed C-130 Hercules support aircraft joins the team and promptly earns the nickname "Fat Albert".
 
October and November 1971:
The team makes their first, and Far East tour, visiting Korea, Japan, Taiwan, the Philippines and Guam.
 
May and June 1973:
The third European tour is comprised of demonstrations in England, France, Spain, Turkey, Iran, and Italy.
 
May 1974: A-4F Skyhawk II
The team begins flying their only attack aircraft, the McDonnell Douglas A-4F Skyhawk II. The first show is at Omaha, Nebraska.

October 8, 1977:
Demonstration number 2000 is flown at NAS Atlanta Georgia.
1987: F/A-18 Hornet
The team makes its most recent aircraft change, picking up the McDonnell Douglas F/A-18 Hornet.

September 7, 1990:
The team flies its 3000th demonstration at Grand Forks, North Dakota.
June 21, 1997:
The team performs at NAS Willow Grove Pennsylvania for the first time in over 10 years. 250,000 fans brave the 92+ degree temperatures, and this web site is conceived.
