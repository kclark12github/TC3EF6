The B-2 Flight Deck Panorama

This QTVR Panorama was taken inside the flight deck of AV-20 (93-1087), the first fully operational Block 30 B-2. AV-20 was located inside U. S. Air Force Plant number 42 Palmdale, Californina. Northrop-Grumman Corporation is the prime contractor for the B-2. Despite having a wingspan nearly two-thirds the length of a football field, the aircraft's radar cross-section is described by the Air Force as "in the insect category."

The AIR&SPACE QTVR camera was mounted between the two pilot's seats, a bit lower than the pilot's head would normally be situated, so as to give the best overall view of the flight deck. At the time this picture was taken, the airplane had some 16 hours of flight time and was in final acceptance testing as the first operational production B-2.

Note: At 28.8 Baud connect speed, expect about a 4 minute download. At 14.4 connect speed, expect about a 8 minute download.
