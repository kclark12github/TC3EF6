Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird

Interesting tidbits...
  • The SR-71 began life as a CIA project designated the A-12...
  • The YF-12 project actually attempted to make this aircraft a fighter (albeit an interceptor)...!
  • The original designation was intended to be RS-71 but President Johnson misidentified the aircraft in a press conference, and rather than correct the President of the United States, they decided to stick with the new SR-71 designation...
  • The Blackbird was retired in 1990, but on its final flight from the Air Force to the Smithsonian, it set the official cross country speed record of 68 minutes and 17 seconds from coast to coast with an average speed of 2112.52 miles per hour.
  • In 1994 Congress appropriated funds to put two aircraft back in service, and these airplanes were taken out of storage, refurbished and redelivered to the USAF. These aircraft were to be operational by December 1996.
