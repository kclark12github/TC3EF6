Select Audiovisual Records

Pictures of United States Navy Ships 1775-1941

National Archives and Records Administration,
Washington, DC 20408,
USA.
This select list describes photographs and photographs of artworks or models of ships that depict types of ships used by the Nayy from the Revolutionary War period to December 7, 1941, before America's entry into the Second World War.

Ships commissioned before the use of steel for ship construction was begun in 1883 are listed under Early Ships subdivided by method of propulsion, and listed thereunder chronologically by date of commissioning. Each entry gives the ship's name; a previous or later name if applicable; the date of commissioning; a description of the ship; the view perspective; information about the original sketch, model, or painting when known; and the picture identification number in italics.

Ships commissioned after 1883 are listed under Modern Ships and are subdivided by general class, such as auxiliary or destroyer, and thereunder chronologically. Each entry gives the ship's name and designator (or just the designator when there is no name); the ship type, where applicable; the photographic perspective and, where applicable, other information; and the date of the original photograph and its identification number.

An alphabetical index to ships has been included to facilitate finding numbered entries for each ship.

To order individual prints, negatives, or slides, write to the Still Picture Branch (NNSP) for a current price list.
