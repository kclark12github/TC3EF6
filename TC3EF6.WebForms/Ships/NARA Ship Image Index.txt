     This select list describes photographs and photographs
of artworks or models of ships that depict types of ships
used by the Nayy from the Revolutionary War period to
December 7, 1941, before America's entry into the Second
World War.
     Ships commissioned before the use of steel for ship con-
struction was begun in 1883 are listed under "Early Ships~'
subdivided by method of propulsion, and listed thereunder
chronologically by date of commissioning. Each entry gives
the ship's name; a previous or later name if applicable; the
date of commissioning; a description of the ship; the view
perspective; information about the original sketch, model,
or painting when known; and the picture identification
number in italics.
     Ships commissioned after 1883 are listed under "Modern
Ships" and are subdivided by general class, such as aux-
iliary or destroyer, and thereunder chronologically Each
entry gives the ship's name and designator (or just the
designator when there is no name); the ship type, where
applicable; the photographic perspective and, where
applicable, other information; and the date of the original
photograph and its identification number.
     An alphabetical index to ships has been included to facili-
tate finding numbered entries for each ship.
     To order individual prints, negatives, or slides, 
write to the Still Picture Branch(NNSP) for a current price list.
 Many photographs of Navy ships are not listed in this leaflet.
 Separate inquiries about other photographs should be as specific
 as possible listing names, dates, places, events, and other
details.
