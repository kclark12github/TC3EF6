A Short Biography


I was born in San Francisco and raised in the San Jose area where I went to High School at Archbishop Mitty and college at De Anza and San Jose State.

I spent four years in the Air Force (again a symptom of my addiction to flying) and learned all about electronics. What a disappointment, in the four years I spent in the Air Force I didn't get to fly in so much as a single Air Force aircraft. Although I was all set to do the training to be able to take a ride in an F-106 when they decided to close the base where I was stationed and everything got canceled.

So, you ask, what is my story? Read on!!

As you may or may not have guessed from reading this page and from the background, I'm GAY! I wasn't always proud of who I am, quite the opposite and I tried very hard for a very long time to be a "straight" man. Sorry honey, it ain't gonna happen.

I had a very strongly opinionated and socially though not racially bigoted father ( I still loved him to death) and macho male influences, not to mention the religious influences when I was growing up. The simple fact of the matter is that I knew I was gay by the time I was fourteen (so I use that as a time reference). Try as hard as I might I couldn't loose or even modify the feelings I had for other guys. I had a succession of girl friends and I was even married for fifteen years.....but I just couldn't deny who I am. It only took twenty-seven years but I finally came out to myself. Once I came out to myself and discovered that I wasn't some sick, perverted person, that in-fact I am one helluva nice guy, I was able to be comfortable with who I am. Soon after that I came out to my now ex-wife (who by the way is still one of my best friends!) and just this last summer (1996) I told my daughter. Courtney just looked at me as if to say, SO?

What do I like to do?

Fly (OBVIOUSLY), backpack, mountain bike, spend time with Courtney , write (which I do for a living). I like to spend a little time at the Billy DeFrank Center in San Jose.

What Do I Want In Life?


To be happy, and to have my health, and to share my life with someone special. Yes, I'm looking for that one special man with whom I can spend the rest of my days. Neither one of is perfect, we have our faults and we know it, but we love each other anyway, maybe even because of those little flaws. One thing we make of point of is reaffirming our love and COMMITMENT daily. We sometimes argue but we "never let the sun set on our anger." We share our dreams and aspirations, and disappointments. We rejoice together in our victories, and cry together in our sorrows, always encouraging and drawing strength from the other.

We love to spend time outdoors, walking hand-in-hand along a beach pausing to sit together and watch the warm golden orb of the sun set slowly below the western horizon. We spend peaceful weekends deep in a cool mountain wilderness or riding our bicycles around town visiting little out of the way shops, pausing for a cup of coffee or an iced tea at a curbside cafe just off an almost unknown alley.

An evening out to dinner and the theater are a special treat topped off by cuddling on huge throw pillows in front of the fireplace and making love for hours before falling asleep in each others arms.

Your day at work might be interrupted by a call from the front desk, the receptionist says you have a delivery. Filled with curiosity you enter the lobby and notice a strangely sweet fragrance. Approaching the desk in the middle of the cathedral like lobby you notice a large bouquet of roses, carnations, and honeysuckle mounted atop the counter next to the receptionist. You ask the receptionist for your package, she points to the flowers and smiles. The card tucked neatly inside says simply "thinking of you my love." Later that evening, after work and a light dinner, we spend a couple of hours running or at the gym.

What am I concerned about?


I suppose the biggest concern right now is gay rights, actually it's more like basic HUMAN rights. Of course, HIV/AIDS and gay youth. I wish I had the kind of support networks available to gay youth today when I was young.
