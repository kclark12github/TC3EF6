USS Enterprise (CVN 65) added to
forces in Persian Gulf

Full-screen .jpg images are hyperlinked from the images captioned in story below, and high resolution, full-size, suitable for print media are hyperlinked from "Hi-Rez" in the captions.

On Sept. 15, the U.S. Navy's nuclear-powered aircraft carrier USS Enterprise (CVN 65) and her battle group transited the Suez Canal as seen here from the starboard wing of the ship's bridge. Enterprise and Carrier Air Wing 17 are now in the Arabian Gulf, along with USS Carl Vinson (CVN 70) and Carrier Air Wing 14, to ensure Iraqi compliance with the no-fly zones. U.S. Navy photo by Lieut. Joe Alvarado. Hi-Rez.		 WASHINGTON, Sept. 19 --The aircraft carrier USS Enterprise (CVN 65) has joined the USS Carl Vinson (CVN 70) in the Arabian Gulf. The Norfolk, Va.-based nuclear-powered aircraft carrier and its more than 70 aircraft arrived in the Gulf today.

Along with Enterprise are the guided missile cruiser USS Gettysburg (CG 64), the attack submarine USS Norfolk (SSN 714), and the combat support ship USS Supply (AOE 6).

The USS Carl Vinson (CVN 70) Battle Group moved into the northern Arabian Gulf at the end of August, responding to escalating activity by Iraqi ground forces. Also responding were ships operating as part of the maritime interception force under the U.S. Fifth Fleet.

Enterprise's commanding officer, Capt. Michael D. Malone, said, "On Sept. 12, we were off the coast of Bosnia and three days later we were in the Red Sea responding to the President's call. This is a fine example of the mobility and flexibility that a forward-deployed aircraft carrier provides for our national security interests."
