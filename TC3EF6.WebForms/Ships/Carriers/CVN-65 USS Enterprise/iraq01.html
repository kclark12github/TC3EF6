USS Enterprise (CVN 65) headed towards Arabian Gulf

WASHINGTON, Sept. 13 --The aircraft carrier USS Enterprise (CVN 65) is heading towards the Suez Canal today in preparation for any action in the Arabian Gulf. The Norfolk, Va.-based nuclear-powered aircraft carrier and its more than 80 aircraft will provide whatever additional capabilities to the forces in the Gulf the President and the Secretary of Defense decide are appropriate.

Along with Enterprise are the guided missile cruiser USS Gettysburg (CG 64), the attack submarine USS Norfolk (SSN 714), and the combat support ship USS Supply (AOE 6).

The ships could join with the USS Carl Vinson (CVN 70) Battle Group that moved into the northern Arabian Gulf at the end of August, responding to escalating activity by Iraqi ground forces, as well as ships operating as part of the maritime interception force under the U.S. Fifth Fleet.

Enterprise is under the command of Capt. Michael D. Malone.
