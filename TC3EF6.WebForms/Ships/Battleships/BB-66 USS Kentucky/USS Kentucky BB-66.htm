History

The keel of KENTUCKY (BB-66), an IOWA-class battleship, was laid at Norfolk Nary Yard, Portsmorth, Va., 6 December 1944. Construction was suspended 17 February 1947 when the battleship was 72.1 percent complete.

The 120-ton, 68-foot-long section of her bow was used to repair the WISCONSIN (BB-64) after a collision with the EATON (DDE-510) on May 6, 1955.

Her name was struck from the Navy List 9 June 1958; and her uncompleted hulk was sold for scrapping to Boston Metals Co., Baltimore, Md., 31 October.
