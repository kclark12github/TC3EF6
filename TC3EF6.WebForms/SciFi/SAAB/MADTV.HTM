Before Saturday Night Live or National Lampoon, there was MAD Magazine. MAD TV has been goofing on fat cats and big wigs, parodying commercials and satirizing movies and TV shows since your parents were cool.

Now there's MAD TV - as brash, bold and witty as the magazine; as hip, hot and edgy as In Living Color, SCTV or the original Saturday Night Live.

MAD TV is irreverent, outrageous, sometimes offensive. It shows no mercy and pulls no punches. Whether it's a sketch about P.C. Cops, a Gump Fiction film parody or a XXX Files spoof about porn stars being abducted by aliens, nothing is off limits for MAD TV.

Plus, brand-new animation created by Klasky/Csupo (The Simpsons, Duckman) brings the classic MAD characters of Don Martin and "Spy vs. Spy" cartoon strips to life.

With an ensemble of the freshest, funniest young actors around and a top-notch production team, MAD TV is breaking new comic ground as only FOX can.

Our cast of MAD TV men and women includes Bryan Callen (Stand Up New York, The Comic Strip) David Herman (House of Buggin'), Orlando Jones (Sinbad, Roc), Phil LeMarr (Living Single, Mad About You), Artie Lange (New York's Live on Tape), Mary Scheer (Seinfeld, Culture Clash), Nicole Sullivan (Madman of the People, Herman's Head) and Debra Wilson (Uptown Comedy Club).

Superstar producers Quincy Jones and David Salzman, whose company QDE created Fresh Prince, In the House and many other hits, heads up a first-rate production team which also includes Fax Bahr & Adam Small (Hearts of Darkness, In Living Color, House of Buggin') and Steven Haft (Dead Poets Society). James Jones (The Ben Stiller Show) produces and John Blanchard of SCTV and Kids in the Hall is the director.
