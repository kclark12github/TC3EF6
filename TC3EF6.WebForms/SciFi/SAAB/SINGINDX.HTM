When you're living in the 90s, living in New York and living single, you need all the help you can get -- like the companionship and support of good friends. Created by Yvette Lee Bowser (producer of A Different World  and Hangin' with Mr. Cooper ), Living Single is a fresh, funny and very real look at six young, upwardly mobile friends sharing the ups and downs of their lives, loves and careers.

Khadijah James (Queen Latifah) is the smart, sophisticated and enterprising founder of Flavor Magazine; Synclaire James (Kim Coles) is her spirited, though sometimes naive, cousin; and Regine Hunter (Kim Fields Freeman) is a chic, social climber whose name fits, because she's usually hunting men. The three share a New York apartment, but they rarely have it to themselves.

Khadijah's best friend is Maxine "Max" Shaw (Erika Alexander), a brash and quick-witted public defender, who is always dropping by.

And when four fly ladies get together, it's not long before the guys upstairs drop by: Kyle Barker (T.C. Carson), a slick, career-minded Wall Street executive and self-proclaimed ladies' man, has an ongoing love/hate relationship with the sassy Max; and Overton Jones (John Henton), the building's wise and witty handyman, is Synclaire's steady boyfriend.

Together, these six share the fun, feuds and frustrations of young adulthood as only true friends can.
