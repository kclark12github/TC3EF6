Dragonsdawn

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

I'm the kind of person who hates having projects hanging over his head. Before leaving on a trip I'll do everything possible to finish up my work so I can start fresh on something new when I return.

This time, I resisted the impulse. I left for the 1987 World SF Convention in Brighton, England, with the painting for Anne McCaffrey's Dragonsdawn still in my studio. It seemed to be finished to me. Usually I would have a messenger take it out of my hands and deliver it to the publisher so I could leave with a free mind. But something held me back.

One of our side trips in the U.K. was to the coast of Wales, and we were having a wonderful day at the spectacular Three Cliff's Bay. At one point, I walked out into the water and looked back to the shore. Instantly, a flaw in the painting that I'd left back home became apparent. I could see how the water should have been rendered in the painting. The way I had painted the scene-- not having a handy beach by my studio to study-- wasn't as convincing. When I returned I repainted that part and then sent it in... with confidence.

There's no substitute for studying the real thing.
