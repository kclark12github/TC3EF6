The Narrow Way

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

As I mentioned elsewhere, the greatest measure of my satisfaction as an illustrator is when I can produce an artwork that reflects my artistic concerns just as much as it illustrated the book. This painting is one of the best examples I have of this happy condition. It is a piece I would have done anyway, and it would stand on its own in a gallery without reference to any book; yet it also works well as a cover for the book, Tanith Lee's novel Death's Master.

The Narrow Way is one of the two paintings I have done outdoors. I felt I needed to paint the mottled tree shadows near real trees throwing their shadows on the ground around me.
