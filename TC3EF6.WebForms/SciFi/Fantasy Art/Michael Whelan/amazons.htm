Amazons

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

Although covers for anthologies don't pay well, they offer as close to complete freedom as a book cover commission can get. Usually I only have to come up with an image that communicates the theme tying the stories together, and then run with it. This makes it much easier to realize my oft-stated goal of creating a work that both satisfies the requirements of the publisher and expresses my own artistic themes.

This painting for the anthology Amazons was the first cover in which I deliberately set out to use a symbolic element. In this case, the moon: a symbol (and source?) of feminine spirit and strength.
