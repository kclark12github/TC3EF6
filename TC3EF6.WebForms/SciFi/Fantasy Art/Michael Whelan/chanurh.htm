Chanur's Homecoming

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

The biggest challenge in painting alien creatures is in finding ways to communicate information about the way they think, via the gestures available to their peculiar forms. Of course the more humanoid the alien creatures, the easier the job, for everyone has an innate knowledge of the subtleties of human nonverbal expression. With non-human creatures though, this can lead to misinterpretations. Take chimps, for example-- they seem to smile when they are afraid and yawn when they are feeling aggressive. If aliens were to meet us humans, even if they were to have humanoid forms, the chances are that each other's expressions would cause both species considerable confusion.

However, in the world of illustration, my goal is to communicate something of the aliens' nature to the viewer. Since my audience is made up of humans, it is fitting for me to use the human library of gesture to convey my point. Such is the case here, in my painting for the novel Chanur's Homecoming, by C.J. Cherryh.
