Dark Tower

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

The winter months of 1980-81 were tough. My wife and I became parents with the arrival of our daughter in November, and I needn't tell anyone who has children what that means in terms of life-style changes... like lack of sleep. A number of coincident upsets and tragedies seemed to beset us then, as well, in addition to a fairly heavy workload and miserably cold weather.

Set against the background was the commission to fully illustrate one of the most unremittingly bleak (though engaging) Stephen King novels I've ever read, The Dark Tower. If the book hadn't been so interesting, it would have been hard to maintain my enthusiasm through the completion of the work.
