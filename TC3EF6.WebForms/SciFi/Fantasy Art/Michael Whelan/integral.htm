Integral Trees

 

From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

Being suspended in space, floating unencumbered yet in control of one's flight, is as close to experiencing pure freedom and tranquility as I can imagine. It seemed inevitable that I would enjoy reading this book and attempting to visualize an entirely credible alien environment based on that weightless feeling. It suggested an infinite number of pictorial possibilities.

As an artist, I am also intrigued by the physics of the lighting in Niven's "Smoke Ring." For example, there is a sharp, cool light source orbiting a warm, diffuse light source. This would mean a constantly shifting color balance and intensity as well as a gradual change from one light source to two, in addition to eclipses with one star over another, or occasional eclipses by clumps of matter in the system, and so on. Given the complexity, I thought it best to portray the Smoke Ring Itself rather than to focus on one character in particular. Though the front cover features a female inhabitant, the painting as a whole is dominated by the central star.
