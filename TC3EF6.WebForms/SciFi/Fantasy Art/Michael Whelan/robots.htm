The Robots of Dawn

 

From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

Sometimes the simplest approach is the best solution to a problem. As a reflection of my great enjoyment of this book, my early concept drawings were complex arrangements of thematic symbols-- my attempts to portray most of the interesting facets of the novel. However, none of these sketches seemed quite right, and as I worked, I found the concepts evolving gradually into more basic compositions. I decided that the pivotal plot element was the robot Giskard, and the most interesting metaphors were of dawn/dusk and trees. So I posed Giskard against a Solarian landscape. Giskard's pose echoes the tree shapes, as he shields his eyes from the light and gazes skyward to the stars where the destiny of man-- and robot-- ultimately awaits.
