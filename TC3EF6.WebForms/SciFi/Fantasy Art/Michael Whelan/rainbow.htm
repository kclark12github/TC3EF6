Passage: The Rainbow

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

The surface meaning of my "Passage" concept is technical in nature: I wish to develop my oil painting technique further so that I am more comfortable with the medium. It signifies a passage from working nearly exclusively in acrylics to attaining a greater technical flexibility in oils. In spite of the resolution, I painted this vision in acrylics because I saw the sky, in a dream, as looking very airbrushed and smooth. It would have been pointless travail, then, to try to capture the atmosphere in oils.

In doing this painting, I altered some details from what I originally had in mind. I'm quite satisfied with it, but I think I'll eventually try to render the original vision more exactly.
