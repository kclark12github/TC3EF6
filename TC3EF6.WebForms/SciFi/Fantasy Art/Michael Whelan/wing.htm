Wing

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

In April 1992, my family and I went traveling in Australia. During the trip I realized how much my daughter had grown and how fast she would be changing from that point on. It's a wonderful but bittersweet emotion, strange and hard to grasp, yes subtly shocking when the realization suddenly comes into focus as it did on our climb up Australia's most famous natural monument.

Up on Ayers Rock, I felt Alexa and I were poised in time between Alexa's childhood and the rest of her life. While on the top, someone dropped a handkerchief, which the strong wind immediately carried away. Sailing off, first seen against the blue sky, and then falling against the vermilion rock faces, I felt what I was experiencing was a sublime vision perfectly timed to coincide with my thoughts and emotions. Later, with this painting I attempted to pull it all together in one image.
