L'échelle

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

An ad agency commissioned me to do a painting for a Waldenbooks promotion. The painting was to be used in a poster campaign, and the artwork itself was to be given away in a sweepstakes. Before I even started coming up with ideas, I agreed to sell the original art and all rights-- something I rarely do.

Then I started work on this painting. As I gradually arrived at this scene, I became more and more enamored of the thought behind it. A story evolved that was good enough to generate at least another painting or two. I could visualize the other works rising out of this one, then a book perhaps, even a movie, who knows!? I panicked: I had sold all the rights.

I ended up buying back out of the contract enough rights to the painting to allow me the option of eventually developing the concepts further. For me, the "free" in "freelance" is all about self direction and keeping one's options open.
