Two Worlds

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

Two Worlds is an oil painting done as a commission for DAW Books, for one of their Year's Best Horror collections. Nevertheless, it's a personal work, really... an allegory on Western disassociation and insulation from the suffering going on in the third world. It abounds in symbolism; the owl, for example, is a classical symbol of wisdom... in this case, though, he is blindfolded and carrying coins in his clutches. "Now what do you suppose Michael means by that?"
