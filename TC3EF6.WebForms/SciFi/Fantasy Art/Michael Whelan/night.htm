Night's Daughter

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

If I don't take the time to keep notes during the creation of a painting, I often forget the meaning of some of the symbolism in it. The plants in this illustration for Tanith Lee's novel  Night's Daughter have some sort of symbolic rationale for their presence in the painting, but I can't for the life of me remember what it is. It was important enough to me when I was doing the painting that I went out looking for a house in the area that I knew was landscaped with some of the flowers seen in the foreground of the image. I recall receiving curious looks from the occupant of the house as I stalked around making sketches and notes about the plants.
