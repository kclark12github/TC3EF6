Foundation and Empire

 

From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

Once again there was a clear choice for the central subject of this cover painting: the clownish "Mule," the one variable Seldon overlooked in his massive plan. outwardly a buffoon, the Mule is the greatest concentration of power in a single individual in the Galaxy, and thus threatens to undo generations of Foundation efforts to restore peace.

In this scene, hopeful dawn gives way to harsh light of midday. The once stately columns of the Empire now lean crazily in broken disarray. Perching over the scene like a comic vulture is the Mule. He is gaudily clad in his harlequin outfit, but his eyes reflect his tragic character and his Achilles heel-- his love for Bayta-- is symbolized by the bright pink cloth tied to his leg.
