The Stone Of Farewell

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan...

The main problem in doing what are called "wraparound" covers-- cover paintings that wrap around from the front to the back of the book-- is in trying to avoid pieces that look like two different paintings stuck together. Sometimes I work hard at wedding the two halves, and other times I like the demarcation between the two sides and I seek to exploit it.

The two sides of this painting flow together. But it obviously contains two main objects of interest vying for attention... there is just enough extra interest on the right to cause the eye to settle on that side, which is fine for western eyes, used to reading from left to right.
