Dragon Fire

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

There is ample evidence on the bookstands for the truth behind the saying "Don't judge a book by its cover." The most important thing I seek as an illustrator is consistency between the art and the text.

In doing the cover art commission for Melanie Rawn's Sunrunner's Fire, I had submitted a color sketch with everything in place, as in the final version, but by mistake the anatomical structure of the dragon's wings was inconsistent with the wings of the dragons on the previous three covers I'd done for the series. For the full-scale painting I corrected the wings to match the earlier ones.

My client liked them better the first way.

I tried to explain that it was important to maintain uniformity between the dragons and the text, but they wanted the wings like the ones in the concept and that was that! My response was to start over entirely and do a painting with the wings designed as in the color sketch. That way, I thought, if a fan remarked unfavorably about the differences I could point to the first version. I sent in the second version, and not one person has commented to me regarding the dragon's wings!
