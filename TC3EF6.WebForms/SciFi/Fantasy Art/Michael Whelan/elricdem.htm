Elric Demonslayer

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

Unlike most of my works, I painted this image for the cover of Michael Moorcock's The Vanishing Tower without the benefit of a detailed preliminary concept. My primary guideline was a rough charcoal sketch. Though I was pleased with the final painting, I was dismayed to realized that the sketch had more of a sense of movement than the finished piece.

I puzzled over the problem for some time before I saw the answer. In the painting, the shape formed by the two combatants is a triangle with its base on the ground plane: the most stable and solid shape of all. When I reexamined the sketch, I saw that I had used a vaguely triangular shape, but the base was tilted, creating a subtle but effective unbalanced feeling more in keeping with the scene.
