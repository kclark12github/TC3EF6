From The Art of Michael Whelan...

The concept of "prudence" is an important theme to me. It doesn't refer to the modern notion of prudence with its uptight moralistic connotations. Instead, it hearkens to the classical notion of the virtue Prudence. That is, possession an accurate knowledge of and ability to learn from the past, a clear perception of the present and where current trends are leading, and the clarity of vision to foresee the results of one's actions.

The inference is that a person who has cultivated the virtue of prudence is a person who has learned from the past and acts in the present so as to ensure an ideal future.
