From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

When Judy-Lynn del Rey asked me to do this assignment she said, "What we're looking for here is a 'big book' look." I had only a vague idea of what she meant (except that "big books" usually do not feature narrative cover art), but I know that the painting I created would have to be different from most others I had done. it was my first Clarke cover, and as 2001 is one of my all-time favorites I thought that 2010 would be a joy to do.

It didn't turn out that way. I ended up having to do ten comps instead of the usual two to four because of Ballantine's reluctance to leave any detail to chance in the marketing of such a potential bestseller. Also, I decided to give the painting a more diffuse, soft-edged quality than I usually want, and this necessitated using an airbrush, which I tend to dislike. Nevertheless, I was pleased with the final result.

An interesting note: in one of those comps I mentioned, I placed the ship at an angle over the Star Child's hands. At a science-fiction convention where I had it on display, someone complimented me on the inspiration of portraying the Discovery spacecraft as the Star Child's baby rattle. I thanked him, but to be honest, though it was an interesting concept it was altogether unintentional!
