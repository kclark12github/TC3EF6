Daughter of Regals & Other Tales

 

From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

A problem in choosing the image for this book is that the young woman in the story is consistently described as being drab or, at best, uninteresting. Yet uninteresting is the last thing a book cover should be! To combat this I had her face away from the light and contrasted the dreariness of her life with the glittering promise of her future, represented by the stained-glass window. There a dragon is seen fixed in the shape of a heart, the centerpoint of her soul's desire.

This is one of my last paintings on masonite, a hard and "unsympathetic" material to paint on, which has been largely replaced in my studio now by canvas and watercolor board.
