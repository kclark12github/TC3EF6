Firestarter

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

After reading Stephen King's Firestarter, there was only one image I could envision for the cover: the moment of supreme realization of the protagonist's power. I used my niece, Laina Madsen, for my model because she fit King's description of his character so well. I originally visualized her with an angry face, so I hung around the house and waited until Laina had an argument with someone. In all the pictures I took of her that day, she looks pretty grouchy! Anyway, in typical fashion, I changed my mind after consulting the text again. A look of trancelike fascination, as if she were enthralled by the experience of her power blossoming into fullness, would be more true to the novel.
