Causeway

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

One cool, foggy morning I went to take pictures at the reservoir near my home. I was standing at the water's edge, and everything was dead silent. The water was a mysterious deep organic green color, and the air was so full of mist that the color of the water seemed to leach out, turn transparent, and fade to nothing before me. There were two ducks out there just beyond my range of vision; I could barely hear the subtle sound of their floating on the water. Then I noticed them smoothly gliding, moving in and out of the mist as if they knew exactly where the line was for me between perception and obscurity. Back and forth they drifted there on the water, in the silence, teasing me visually, looking for all the world like apparitions shimmering in and out of reality. It was a magical moment and the feeling inspired me when I was creating this painting.
