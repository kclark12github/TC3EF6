From Michael Whelan's Works of Wonder...

My first challenge in doing a painting for this novel was to decide whether or not to attempt to fit the cover concept into the unifying scheme of my first four Foundation covers. The planet Trantor would not be an appropriate background for this cover-- though it appears on the previous covers-- since it is not a part of this story. Yet I felt some of the already developed symbolism worked well and could be applied to this painting, too. So what I ended up with was a "yes-and-no" answer to my problem: no backdrop featuring Trantor, but some symbolic links to the other covers.

The most obvious of these links was to be the moon, which is very important to the story and also could be represented in the circular shape used in the previous covers to represent the galaxy and its destiny. All save one of the sketches I submitted to the art director used the moon in the sky as a background element. Of course, that single moonless sketch was the one that was chosen!

Fortunately, the scene from the book that the painting illustrated involved a character wearing a spacesuit; the bubble helmet provided the circular element that I was looking to include in the rendering. The scene also describes the discovery of a long-lost civilization. What I added is the model of the statue: none other than the heroic robot Giskard. Daneel's friend and closest ally, Giskard is ultimately the primary reason for humanity's exodus into space-- thus the field of stars around his head.
