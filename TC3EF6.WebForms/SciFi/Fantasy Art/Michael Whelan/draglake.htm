Dragon Lake

 

From The Art of Michael Whelan

"Some come easy, some come hard," said Norman Rockwell, when someone asked him how long it took for him to complete a painting. Like him, I'm somewhat of a perfectionist. Often, after I've made my plans, it takes me twice as many work hours as I allotted to complete an assignment. It's hard to stay awake into the late nights week after week; one of the ways I keep going is by listening to recorded books. When I'm almost through a painting, just rendering embellishments and details, I enjoy listening to the tapes-- as I did while completing this cover painting for Melanie Rawn's The Star Scroll. This practice has a long and honorable history: such luminaries as Rockwell and even Leonardo himself employed people to read to them while they painted.
